Call for Papers

This year, in addition to the Main track, we are introducing the Encore track for the first time.

Main track

Relevant topics

LSGDA 2025 invites submissions on a wide range of topics related to large-scale graph data analytics, including but not limited to:

  • Graph data models, storage, indexing, and query processing techniques
  • Graph mining techniques
  • Distributed graph analytics techniques
  • Graph visualization techniques and system interfaces
  • Dynamic and streaming graph data analytics
  • Spatiotemporal graph analytics
  • Machine learning techniques for graphs
  • Industry & Application Papers on graph analytics in domains such as social networks, multimedia, semantic web, biological data, business processes, transportation data, and more
  • Vision, Experiment, and Survey Papers covering graph data analytics, experimental methodologies, benchmarking studies, and future research directions

Submission Categories

LSGDA 2025 accepts the following types of papers:

  • Regular Research Papers (up to 8 pages, excluding references)
  • Industry & Application Papers (up to 8 pages, excluding references)
  • Vision Papers (up to 8 pages, excluding references)
  • Experiment & Benchmark Papers (up to 8 pages, excluding references)
  • Survey Papers (up to 8 pages, excluding references)

Submissions must be original and must not be under review in any other venue. Each paper will be evaluated based on originality, significance, technical quality, relevance to LSGDA, and presentation. At least one author of each accepted paper must register for VLDB 2025 (workshop or full conference registration) and is expected to present the paper in person. Online presentation requests require approval from the organizing committee.

Important Dates

  • Full Paper Deadline: 15 June, 2025  30 May, 2025
  • Author Notification: 3 July, 2025  25 June, 2025; 16 June, 2025
  • Camera-Ready Deadline:  10 July, 2025  30 June, 2025

All submission deadlines follow the Anywhere on Earth (AoE) time zone(UTC-12) .

Submission Instructions

Accepted papers will be published in the VLDB Workshop Proceedings.

  • Submissions must be in PDF format and written in English.
  • Authors must use the official VLDB proceedings template and adhere to the :  VLDB 2025 Formatting Guidelines.
  • Industry, vision, experiment, and survey papers must be clearly labeled in their title, e.g., "Industry Paper: [Title]".
  • LSGDA follows a single-blind review process, meaning that author names, affiliations, and contact details must be included in the submission.

NEW!! Encore Track

To highlight recent and relevant research in graph analytics, LSGDA 2025 introduces the Encore Track to facilitate knowledge dissemination and collaboration. Researchers may submit an abstract, title, and author information of recently published work at prestigious venues for an encore presentation at LSGDA.

Eligible submissions must have been published, in press, or accepted for publication since January 1, 2024 in well-regarded conferences (e.g., SIGMOD, VLDB, ICDE, EDBT, KDD, CIKM) or journals (e.g., IEEE TKDE, VLDB Journal, ACM TODS). Authors of accepted Encore Track submissions will be invited to present their work as lightning talks at the workshop.

Encore Track Submission & Review Process

The acceptance decision will be based on both quality and relevance to LSGDA.

  • Submissions will be reviewed on a rolling basis.
  • Authors must submit a PDF copy of the published paper and the official URL to the publisher"s version.
  • If the paper has been accepted but is not yet published, authors must also provide evidence of acceptance (e.g., acceptance notification email in PDF format).

Important Dates (Encore Track)

  • Notification: Within 14 working days of submission
