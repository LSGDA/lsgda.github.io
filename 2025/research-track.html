Call for Papers

Relevant topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Graph data model, storage, indexing and query processing techniques
  • Graph mining techniques
  • Techniques for distributed graph analytics
  • Graph visualization techniques and system interfaces
  • Dynamic and streaming graph data analytics
  • Spatial-temporal graph analytics
  • Machine learning techniques for graphs
  • Industry papers about graph analytics in various application domains such as social networks multimedia, semantic web, biological data, business processes, transport data, etc.
  • Vision papers to survey the area of graph data analytics as well as describe the future research directions

Instructions

  • Submitted papers must be original contributions and cannot be under review for any other forum.
  • Each paper will be judged on its originality, significance, technical quality, relevance to LSGDA, and presentation. Accepted papers will form a volume to be published in the CEUR-WS series.
  • Submissions must be in the form of PDF: Research full papers, vision papers, and industry papers are all limited to 8 pages (two columns), including all figures and tables. There are no restrictions on additional pages containing only references.
  • All submitted papers must be presented in English. Authors should use CEUR-WS proceeding templates, either for LaTeX or for Word, for the preparation of their papers. You can also download an offline version with the style files from http://ceur-ws.org/Vol-XXX/CEURART.zip. It also contains DOCX template files. Please choose 2-column style and do not forget to update the information in the footnote of first page, such as the copyright year. Industry and vision papers should be marked in their title. For instance, "Industry Paper: Your Original Title".
  • LSGDA follows single blind review, which means that authors' information (full name, affiliation, email address) must be included in the submission. CEUR-WS also encourages authors to include their homepages and ORCIDs in their papers.

Important Dates

  • Full Paper Deadline:  30 May, 2025
  • Author Notification:  16 June, 2025
  • Camera-Ready Deadline:  30 June, 2025

All submission deadlines are at 23:59 in Australian Eastern Standard Time (UTC+10).

Submission Review Process

At least one author of every accepted paper is expected to attend the workshop and give an oral presentation. We will conduct the workshop as a hybrid event. Hence, presenters and participants may participate in-person in London or remotely.
